01 Jul 2021

I don’t remember the exact reason that I decided to give the guitar a shot. Probably my feeling was that it’s provides the best ratio of impact to effort of any instrument. You can have played piano or violin for years, yet an audience will still gravitate to a dudebro with 6 months of guitar experience. Plus, guitars are often just lying around. It’s quite rare to stumble across a piano that you can just play. So guitar felt like a useful skill to have.

I bought the cheapest electric guitar (Squier Strat Pack + FM10G) from a nearby musical shop during covid. The idea with an electric guitar was twofold:

  • use headphones to minimise noise in apartment living,
  • try rocksmith, a PC game that you can use to make learning guitar more fun.

Even looking back, this plan seems sound. But guitar really didn’t appeal to me and I gave it up after only a few short weeks. I regret not giving it more of a chance, but I wasn’t in the best place during covid and I’m not surprised that I didn’t have the mental discipline to stick with things.

Overall it was a roughly 500$ experiment. Given how little time I spent on it, this was a little wasteful. I don’t remember how much I sold it for, perhaps 100-200$.
