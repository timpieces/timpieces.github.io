01 May 2024

Over the past few years I’d had many people get into BJJ. The way they talked about it led me to believe that there was ‘something to it’. As if it provides some physical enjoyment that is hard to explain in words. I found this was true of starting at the gym, and thought that BJJ might be similar (but perhaps with added adrenaline).

I signed up for a starter trial course at a local BJJ gym. The course was one month, and you could attend multiple times per week. I attended around six classes over the month.

Towards the end of the month, I was having some serious lower back pain at work. It was constant, and stuck around whether I was sitting or standing. I went to the physio, who diagnosed me with tight QL muscles.

It’s not actually a clear causal link between BJJ and the back pain. It’s just as likely that it was something I had done at the gym through undertraining/overtraining certain muscles. But given the timing, and the fact that the gym adds more to my life than BJJ does, I decided to be conservative and stop BJJ.

Looking back, I don’t really regret it, but perhaps I didn’t get far enough into it to get to the point of regret. BJJ seems like a sport that one has to obsess over to enjoy, and I’m not in a phase of my life where I’m all-in on any particular endeavour.

BJJ is one of the few things that I tried that I’d consider a ‘failure’. I don’t regret trying it, as there’s no way that I could have known that beforehand, and things may have turned out different if I hadn’t gotten injured. But given the outcome, it’s hard not to consider this wasted time.
