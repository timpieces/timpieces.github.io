01 Jan 2017

childhood

I did a year or two of keyboard lessons in primary school. I don’t remember much about it except that I hardly practiced outside of lessons (every poor student’s downfall). I gave it up either because I was no longer enjoying it or my parents didn’t like paying for something that I wasn’t practicing (reasonable).

university

I had an itch to pick it back up again during my gap year, and I continued playing during university. I bought a second hand piano from an online marketplace for a few hundred dollars. I decided not to pay a teacher (mostly because I’m cheap). And while I think I wasn’t so consistent, I actually clocked quite a few hours of practice over the years. I learned some Chopin and some Scott Joplin, and improved my music theory a lot. I even practiced scales and did some other drills. I never really had the discipline to get ‘properly good’ or perform, but I got a lot of value out of learning and practicing the basics. Definitely enough that I don’t require the expenses involved or the time spent. I gradually practiced less and less over the coming years, and sold the piano when I moved away from Perth.

It’s not unlikely that I’ll pick up piano again some day, likely with the same attitude of playing for fun.
