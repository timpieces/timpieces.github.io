01 May 2024

I bought a crochet kit from a bookstore in Singapore. It was a Harry Potter kit. I completely butchered Harry but it was enough to get started.

I figured that the best way to learn would be to learn with other people. As a frequent user of Meetup, I resolved to finally create my own group and see what it’s like to be an organiser. As silly as it sounds, this was quite a big step for me. I’d never been good at organising social events, and had been brought up to be quite horrified of this idea.

After some brainstorming ‘City Crochet Escape’ was born. The idea was to try and target young professionals for some after work crochet. I knew it was a long shot at the time.

Our first meetup was at a cafe near my apartment. We had three attendees, all of whom were middle aged women. I was quite happy with this. They were easy to chat to and it proved to me that it wasn’t so hard to organise a meetup after all.

We held a few more meetups, with a similar attendance. Our last meetup had no attendees and so the group naturally faded. Still, I found this a very valuable experience and look upon it very fondly.

Unfortunately, crochet skills are completely non-transferrable skills, but I feel like I could crochet something useful in a bind. I learned a lot about organising social events.
