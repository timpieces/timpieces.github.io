01 Jan 2019

I’m a little remiss to include Rocket League as an endeavour. It was a time in my life where I wasn’t very happy, and didn’t know how to be happy. The time was not well spent. It was very much an escape and way to avoid actually improving my life. But I figure it’s worth documenting something that I spent over 1000 hours on.

I played multiplayer a handful of times in university and found the mechanics a lot of fun. I think that the game has a good balance of teamwork, mechanical skill (i.e. muscle memory and pushing buttons at the right time), and tactics. It’s skill curve keeps you addicted. Most importantly its short games meant you could iterate very quickly with quick feedback. In the same way as chess, I got addicted to starting a game, winning or losing, and then immediately playing again to try to improve. As a result, I believe I cracked into Diamond league 1v1 before I stopped playing.

There are worse ways to spend your time, but I look back at all of the time spent on it and can’t help but think of what else I could have learned. I’m conflicted over whether I enjoyed this time. I think the game is a lot of fun, but I look back on 1000 hours of playing and I don’t remember a single game. It doesn’t fit my retrospective definition of fun.
