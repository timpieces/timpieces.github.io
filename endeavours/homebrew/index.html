10 Sep 2025

At least in Australia, it seems that every middle-aged man tries brewing his own beer at some point in his life. With the knowledge that I’m likely to follow in that path one day, but that I have some time off of work in 2025, I figured why not get it out of the way now?

I bought a kit online, and a fridge to control the fermentation temperature in Singapore’s yeast-unfriendly weather. The kit was a partial mash, and I enjoyed the brewing process, so I found my local homebrew store and bought some more gear and ingredients for another. After the first two brews, I tried to make some cider with a friend. We bought some cheap green apples from the supermarket and manually cut, blended, and squeezed them. Despite the very adhoc process, the cider actually turned out quite decent (but very sour).

It’s a hobby that I’m still very much in the process of pursuing, but so far I’ve found it fun and rewarding, particularly given that it’s not a significant time investment.
