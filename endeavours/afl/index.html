01 Jan 2002

I played Aussie Rules Football (a.k.a. AFL = Australian Football League) for a year or two during primary school. I don’t remember much about it besides that I didn’t have any real talent for it. I started playing young, but a year or two behind others. This meant I had less in-game confidence which leads to getting the ball less which leads to less confidence and the spiral continues. I imagine that this is a common problem for kids in team sports. As a kid it’s hard to have the awareness to diagnose these types of problems. On the plus side, I seemed to have plenty of out-of-game confidence, and the sport helped to develop my social skills. I remember having a positive time overall playing the sport because I enjoyed being around my team members.
