01 May 2025

A good friend that I met in Singapore decided to move to the UK. It was a shame, but as an expat in Singapore it’s expected.

As a going away celebration, I joked that I would teach him how to act in a British pub. He’s from the US and doesn’t drink, and the thought was that he’d have to get comfortable in British pubs as it’s such a significant part of the culture.

I thought this would be a good opportunity to try out Claude Code, so I set it to built a revealjs website with 4 lectures. Each lecture was on a different aspect of British pub culture.

To be clear, this was not slop. I provided a lot of direction to Claude Code and read through every edit. It came out to a presentation that I was very happy with and I presented to him in person. The nice thing about it being a website is that he could revisit it easily and also his partner in the UK could see it.

The main point of the presentation wasn’t so much the tech aspect, but the fact that it gave an excuse to meet up in person each week for four weeks before he left the country. For this reason I think this project was quite a success.
