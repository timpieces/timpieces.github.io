learning programming

Created Dec 1, 2009 - Last updated: Dec 1, 2009

programming learning enjoyed-yes success-yes

(Note: I wasn’t sure how to date this entry, as I’ve never really stopped programming. I decided to focus it around my initial year or two of learning programming as it provides the most insight into the endeavour. I may split out more endeavours as it’s been such an important part of my life)

I remember as a young teenager always wanting to understand how technology really worked. In previous decades, I think that this led people to become fascinated with cars. I had been playing games on our family’s computer since I was 6, so I gravitated towards computers.

I begged my parents for a laptop for my 13th birthday. They relented and through a combination of my allowance and birthday money I was able to buy a 600AUD laptop.

In a quest to better understand how my computer worked, I started trying to learn the C programming language. I remember it being quite painstaking. I started reading through K&R C and experimenting. This was before youtube, before LLMs, before discord, etc. I’m sure there were some IRC channels I could have joined for help, but it’s not something I was aware of as a ~14 year old in 2007. Perhaps no youtube was an advantage at the time; I’m not sure I’d have learned to program if I could be watching nonsense on youtube all day.

Somehow I built up an ability to just keep trying things until they worked. If they didn’t work I’d research as much as possible and try things. The analogy I used was ‘bashing my head against the wall until the wall fell down’. This has been one of the most useful skills I’ve ever learned. While I’m sometimes accused of taking too long to consult others for help, I always get to the root of the issue and learn a lot in the process. The learning makes me more efficient in the future.

I focused on Windows programming at the time as I wasn’t yet at the point where I as trying out Linux (I don’t think I got my first Ubuntu disc until late high school). I got quite familiar with the Windows C API and was building GUIs by hand. I knew nothing of the tooling available on Windows at the time. My first IDE was DevC++ (which even at the time was likely obsolete and did no favours for Windows developers).

I’ll try to detail some of the projects that I worked on at this time in other places e.g. Padman.

Out of all the endeavours that I have tried, self-learning programming at such a young age definitely had the most profound impact on the path of my life.
