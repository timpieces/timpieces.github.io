pen spinning

Created Jan 1, 2008 - Last updated: Jan 1, 2008

novelty enjoyed-yes success-no

I have no idea how to categorise this. Art? Hobby? Skill? Novelty? Let’s try novelty.

This is by far the nerdiest endeavour I’ve ever done (nothing wrong with that!).

I saw a video somewhere online of someone doing fancy tricks with a pen and I was fascinated. I was also the kind of kid who was into juggling and had to learn how to flip over casino chips after watching Casino Royale, so I was very liable to get hooked on this sort of thing.

I practiced a lot and annoyed the people around me for the next few years dropping pens on the desk all the time. Looking back I actually think it’s quite a wholesome hobby, but at the time it was very fringe and I was rather embarassed about it.

I joined the online pen spinning community at the ‘UPSB’ online forum, and posted for a few years as user ‘Tim’. I remember it being quite a safe and welcoming community with plenty of smart people. I’ve heard it wasn’t always like this, I suppose I got lucky or was ignorant. One thing that was useful about being in online forums in the late 2000s was that it was one of the few chances for a young teenager to interact with adults (and other teens) without being treated as a non-adult. People in online forums were just treated as people, and as someone who really disliked the lack of empowerment that comes with being a teenager, I really enjoyed this.

I was a very average pen-spinner, and I only participated in one video. I organised a video for people who had been learning for one year. I think the theme was just an excuse to make a video that I could participate in. The video is still online (youtube is amazing at archiving) and can be seen here:

My webcam (a PS2 eye-toy with some shady PC drivers) has terrible quality and my own video turned out terrible. Still, I look back fondly on having put in the effort to organise others and make the video.
