3d printing

Created Sep 30, 2025 - Last updated: Sep 30, 2025

creativity craft enjoyed-yes success-yes

I’ve been living in the digital world for large parts of my life. 3D printing felt like a good bridge to the physical world. I bought a 3d printer in mid-2025. The main purpose was to learn a little about 3d modelling and a little about 3d printing; enough to get the ‘80% of projects’ that I’d want to be able to do.

So far I’ve been finding the hobby a lot of fun. I’ve set a rough goal to do one print per day. I’ve got a bambu labs a1 which has been very stable.

My biggest surprise has been just how easy it is. It really does feel like a glimpse of the future where ‘postage’ is a thing of the past and you can just print anything that you need at home from some raw materials.

For CAD, I’ve been learning freecad. I’ve found it extremely clunky, but the alternatives are uninspiring in other ways (restrictive licensing, annoying cloud dependencies, etc). I expect that similar to programming, the exact tool doesn’t matter much until you get to expert level.

Notable Prints

Camera Logo Phone Case

I organise a photowalk with my friend. When starting out, I bought a cheap phone case that looked like a camera. It was a fun prop. But it became useless after two months when I bought a new phone. I figured I’d try to print a new one of a custom design.

For our marketing, we had ChatGPT generate a logo that we’ve been using. The goal was to design a phone case in the shape of our logo.

Toothbrush Holder

I came up with the idea of using our building as a toothbrush holder a year or two ago. It’s well suited as our building has two large holes running down the center of the residential floors at the top. This project was mostly to satisfy the itch of achieving something that I had mentioned over a year ago. It’s a nice feeling when the projects you mentioned go from “oh you will never do that” to “oh you did it, it just took longer than expected”.

Toy Chess Set

This was a quick design. For my robot arm I was wanting to do some computer vision to detect chessboards. I didn’t own a chessboard, and so I thought that printing a small one would be useful. I spent quite a while trying to design a way for it to fast together without screws, as I want to be able to print the board modularly. This meant that I’d be able to make the board bigger when I wanted to and everything could fit on the print bed. I wasn’t very happy with the fastening mechanism that I used, but it worked ok.

Carboy Stopper Holder

I started brewing recently. Brewing provides a minor source of inspiration for 3d printing projects (good: plenty of physical equipment and processes. bad: processes involve high temperatures).

One issue I had was that during the fermentation process, sometimes the CO2 release would make the stopper come out of the carboy. To prevent this, I designed and printed a carboy holder that fits around the rim of the carboy and keeps the stopper in place. The image shows an early prototype, the end product looks much nicer and had some nifty 3d printed screws to keep the halfs together.

Squat Rack

I got home from the gym one day and felt like modelling a squat rack for some 3d modelling practice.

This actually evolved into also modelling a little torso that could bench press the barbell. It was a little mechanism with two levers and a cam (on each side) that could lift a barbell up and down. It was actually kind of neat. But once it ‘worked’ I got distracted and didn’t do the necessary work to turn it into something that look nice. In any case, one can still appreciate this mini squat rack.

Trolley Problem

How can you test that people coming into your house aren’t morally bankrupt? It’s not easy.

My partner and I were talking about the trolley problem for some reason, and it occurred to me as something that I could probably design and print. I did a small amount of research on how train track switching works (read: I looked at some images on google images) and got to work. I spent a lot of time modelling this one, particularly the switch itself, as it essentially requires printing a calibrated plastic spring, and this isn’t trivial to do, especially in PLA. I did a basic grid search to find a shape that would work well.

I’m happy to announce that this track complete works! You can toggle the track and the trolley will go either left or right. As per most projects that one undertakes, once the hard part was done, spending the time grinding away to finish it off (i.e. adding more track and some people) fell by the wayside. Still, I’m very happy with how this project turned out and I had good feedback from it.

Mechanisms

This is just a sampling of different mechanisms that I’ve tried to print and design over the past few months. One of these is a planetary gearbox. One is a lead screw mechanism (that I was hooking up a nema17 stepper to). The last one is some timing belt tension jig that doesn’t work well at all.

Different people find 3d printing fun in different ways. For me, I’ve found the most enjoyment from printing functional moving parts. There’s something very satisfying about it.
