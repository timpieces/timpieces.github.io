tennis

Created Jan 1, 2021 - Last updated: Jan 1, 2021

sport social enjoyed-yes success-no

I had two major phases of playing tennis:

  • My parents took us to weekend tennis practice as kids
  • Some friends in Sydney needed a fourth for doubles, so I joined them for casual games and post-game drinks

As a kid I had some minor amount of talent, but I never pursued it seriously. I don’t remember how long we took the weekend classes for, but it was at most a year or two. There are some minor regrets here. While I don’t think tennis is a very well-designed sport (at least when it comes to playing for fun), I think it’s a great outlet for kids to learn about competition. Australia has a lot of infrastructure around it and a big tennis following and community. As someone who struggled to understand the mechanics of team sports, there’s a good chance that I’d have responded well to a competitive individual sport. And it likely would have helped the fact that I was an overweight kid too. That being said, I don’t think our parents had a lot of money to put us through something like tennis lessons unless we showed some significant passion. I think this is a reasonable decision.

I picked tennis up again in Sydney when a friend and his colleagues needed a fourth player. Their previous fourth player was moving away. We played many times over the course of a few years. None of us were any good and the games were mostly determined by unforced errors. Still, it was a great social outlet when I didn’t have many friends at the time.
