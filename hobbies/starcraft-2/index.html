starcraft 2

Created Jan 1, 2012 - Last updated: Jan 1, 2012

strategy games enjoyed-yes success-yes

Much of my high school evenings were spent playing starcraft 2. I don’t feel ashamed about it as I didn’t feel like there was a high opportunity cost to this time. Not to say I couldn’t have been doing better things, but I wasn’t mature enough to actually be doing anything better.

By far the most important thing that I learned from starcraft 2 was the competitive spirit. Not having done much sports up until this point, I had never felt very competitive. Starcraft 2 is the tightest competitive loop that one can experience. You start a game and play 1v1 for 20 minutes. You win or lose, then you start another game. There is some luck involved, but the game is mostly deterministic.

In learning the competitive spirit, I learned more about how skill curves work. After the first ~100 hours of an activity, your improvements become much smaller. But many small improvements slowly add up. In the end, a professional player often isn’t doing ‘better’ things than you are, they are just doing fewer things wrong. This model of the skill curve is widely applicable, and I’m not sure where I’d have learned it if not from starcraft 2.

The competitive scene was fun to follow, and it gave me the feeling of ‘sports is fun to watch’ that I never felt with traditional sports. But this was never a lasting impact of playing the game.

After years of play, I finally broke into the masters league in APAC. I stopped playing shortly after this. I tried to find my Battle.net account to get a screenshot of my rank, but it’s been too difficult to track down.
