programming robotics enjoyed-yes success-yes

I attended a conference in October 2025 where I played chess against a robot. It was a fun distraction, but overall I was a little unimpressed because:

  • the robot arm was slow
  • it kept saying my moves were invalid (they weren’t)
  • it was relatively expensive (1000$)
  • it required playing on a special board

I wanted to try my hand at building a simple chess robot. In principle it didn’t seem too hard, although I’m aware that these things always take longer than we would like. In any case, I’d been wanting to do an electronics and robotics project for some time, so why not give it a shot?

Through the process, I really improved my knowledge of robotics and 3d printing / modelling. Spending so much time thinking about the different design tradeoffs when you don’t have much experience is a great way to learn.

As of November 2025, I have a working pen plotter robot, that I intend to write more about in future! Stay tuned.
